Studies

I am enrolled in the Diploma for Applied AI and Analytics, also known as DAAA. I decided to join this course because I have an interest in technology. Right now, I am taking 7 modules, and some of my favourite modules are Fundamentals of Programming and Front-End Web Development. In these modules, I learn how to code using JavaScript, HTML and CSS. I am currently not drowned in my work and enjoying studying at Singapore Polytechnic.

Food

There are 6 food courts at SP, and every week I usually go to Food Court 4 and 5. Food Court 4 is Koufu, where there are many varieties of food. My favourite food to eat is the Spicy Chicken Set from the Korean Food Stall. However, Koufu is very crowded most of the time and on Thursdays, as I have a longer lunch break, I go to Food Court 5, which is further from my classrooms. I mainly eat Subway and Brunch Cafe, which sell Japanese rice bowls.

CCA

For my CCA, I already knew what I was joining before I came to SP. I joined SPRITE, which is the Singapore Polytechnic Robotics Innovation Technology Enterprise. I joined because I had prior experience in secondary school. In SPRITE, I use LEGO to build robots to complete a specific task, and then competing against other schools. Winning the local competition can allow me to represent Singapore on the international stage, which I have done previously and would love to do again.

Courses Offered in School of Computing
Common ICT Programme

The Common ICT Programme (DICTP) is a one semester programme to help you decide which IT course to take.

This programme offers fundamental IT modules to let you find out what interests you. It includes common foundational modules and exposure to various areas of IT through Infocomm Professional Seminars. After this semester, you can then choose to join the courses mentioned below.

Diploma in Applied AI and Analytics

If you are interested to develop the next Artificial Intelligence (AI) application that benefits our world, this course is for you!

AI & data analytics are the key under-pinning technologies for transforming every industry, including manufacturing, healthcare, and more. You get to face new challenges and projects closely related to solving real-world problems using AI. To facilitate better learning, this course has specially designed AI computer labs which provide students with high performing computers.

Diploma in Infocomm Security Management

Being a Diploma in Infocomm Security Management (DISM) student gives you the competitive edge for a boost in your future studies and career.

To prepare you for both further studies and working life, the DISM curriculum covers the important areas of security technology, security management, information technology, law and teamwork and communications. There is also a Cyber Wargame Center, where realistic scenarios are created to prepare you for real cyberthreats!

Diploma in Information Technology

Information Technology (IT) is the driving force behind many digital transformations we see and use today, including the Smart Nation vision.

The Diploma in Information Technology (DIT) course is positioned to focus on Software & Applications development with three specialist tracks:

  • Software Development
  • User Experience
  • Immersive Simulation
